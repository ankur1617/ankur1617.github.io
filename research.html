Research
My research focuses on understanding how elemental nutrient availability—particularly sulfur— regulates cyanobacterial physiology and toxin production in freshwater ecosystems.
My doctoral work integrates physiological measurements, ionomics, and gene expression analyses to identify mechanistic controls on microcystin biosynthesis in Microcystis aeruginosa.
Current Research Themes
• Sulfur-mediated regulation of microcystin biosynthesis in cyanobacteria
• Harmful algal blooms (HABs) and freshwater toxicology
• Elemental stoichiometry and nutrient–toxin coupling
• Environmental stressors and physiological trade-offs in phytoplankton
