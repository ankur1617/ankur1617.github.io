Research

Research Overview

My research examines how elemental nutrient availability—particularly sulfur—regulates cyanobacterial physiology and toxin production in freshwater ecosystems. I work at the interface of ecological stoichiometry, molecular biology, and environmental toxicology to resolve nutrient–toxin coupling in harmful algal blooms (HABs).

My doctoral work integrates physiological measurements, ionomics, transcriptomics, and toxin quantification to identify mechanistic controls on microcystin biosynthesis in Microcystis aeruginosa, with implications for ecosystem health and water management.

Current Research Themes
  • Sulfur-mediated regulation of microcystin biosynthesis
  • Elemental stoichiometry and nutrient–toxin coupling
  • Environmental stressors and cyanobacterial physiology
  • Ionomics-driven mechanisms in harmful algal blooms
