Research Overview
Research

My research examines how elemental nutrient availability — particularly sulfur — regulates cyanobacterial physiology and toxin production in freshwater ecosystems. I work at the interface of ecological stoichiometry, molecular biology, and environmental toxicology to resolve nutrient–toxin linkages in harmful algal blooms (HABs).

My doctoral work integrates physiological measurements, ionomics, and gene expression analyses to identify mechanistic controls on microcystin biosynthesis in Microcystis aeruginosa, with implications for ecosystem health and water management.
