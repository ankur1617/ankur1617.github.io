Ankur Biswas

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Integrative Biology at Oklahoma State University, working at the intersection of aquatic ecology, environmental chemistry, and toxicology. My dissertation examines sulfate availability as a regulator of toxin production in cyanobacterial harmful algal blooms (cHABs).

Combining physiology, elemental ionomics, and transcriptomics with national-scale environmental datasets (EPA National Lakes Assessment, ~2,751 sites), I show that sulfate concentration predicts microcystin risk independently of bloom biomass — a result with direct implications for water-quality monitoring and public health.
