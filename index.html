About

I am a third-year Ph.D. student at Oklahoma State University. My research focuses on harmful algal blooms, cyanobacterial toxin production, and the role of elemental stoichiometry—particularly sulfur—in regulating ecological and toxicological processes.

I am originally from West Bengal, India, and I am interested in integrating molecular biology, ionomics, and environmental toxicology to address pressing aquatic ecosystem challenges.

Research

  • Sulfur metabolism and microcystin biosynthesis in cyanobacteria
  • Harmful algal blooms (HABs) and freshwater toxicology
  • Elemental stoichiometry, ionomics, and environmental stressors

Teaching

Graduate Teaching Assistant for undergraduate biology and ecology laboratories, with an interest in concept-driven and quantitative biology curricula.
