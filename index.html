Investigating Nutrient–Toxin Interactions in Freshwater Systems

I am a Ph.D. student in Integrative Biology at Oklahoma State University, working at the intersection of ecological stoichiometry, environmental toxicology, and molecular biology. My research focuses on harmful algal blooms (HABs), with particular emphasis on sulfur-driven regulation of cyanobacterial physiology and toxin production.

ABOUT

I received my undergraduate training in India and am broadly interested in bridging atom-level biological processes with ecosystem-scale outcomes. My long-term goal is to develop predictive, mechanistic frameworks for understanding and managing freshwater toxic events under global change.
